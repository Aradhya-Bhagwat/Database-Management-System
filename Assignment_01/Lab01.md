# Lab Assignment 01: Entity-Relationship (ER) Diagram Design

## Objective
To model real-world business requirements by designing high-quality, normalized Entity-Relationship (ER) Diagrams. This includes identifying entities, defining their attributes (primary keys, composite, multi-valued, and derived), and establishing appropriate relationships with proper cardinality and participation constraints.

## Problem Statements

### Task 1: Enterprise System Modeling (ER Diagram 1)
Design and construct a comprehensive ER Diagram representing the data requirements of an enterprise/office database domain.
- Identify all necessary entity sets (e.g., Employees, Departments, Projects) and their respective attributes.
- Distinguish between primary keys, candidate keys, and descriptive attributes.
- Establish relationships, identifying their degrees (binary, ternary) and structural constraints (cardinality ratio and participation).

### Task 2: Logistics & Shipment System (ER Diagram 2)
Design and construct a detailed ER Diagram for a logistics, parts supply, and shipment tracking system.
- Map the relationships between suppliers, parts, projects, and shipments.
- Model multi-way relationship sets or binary approximations as appropriate.
- Clearly annotate structural constraints (e.g., one-to-many, many-to-many) and key constraints.
